How We Clean Carpet

All of our carpet cleaning packages begin with a 3 step dry soil removal process. First we pile lift and comb out hair and debris. This step opens up the fibers to allow our dual motor self adjusting HEPA filtered commercial vacuum to be more productive. After a slow and thorough vacuuming, we detail the edges and corners.

Our base clean is the Standard Clean, which is a low moisture carpet cleaning method using soil emulsifying and encapsulating agents which are agitated and extracted using cotton bonnets. It's a bit like a giant cotton towel blotting your carpet clean using a three directional carpet scrubbing machine. The benefits to this system are that your door stays closed during the cleaning process, your carpets dry within 2 or 3 hours, and there is much less chance of returning spots.

For heavier soiled carpets we use Hot Water Extraction, traditionally known as steam cleaning. The benefits of hot water extraction are heat to accelerate the cleaning agent and the water/vacuum flushing action to quickly remove bulk soil. This is the traditional truck mounted cleaning you've probably seen before

We find that following a Hot Water Extraction cleaning with a low moisture encapsulation cleaning provides all of the benefits of each style of cleaning while negating many of the negative drawbacks. We offer the Double Decker for situations where we feel the carpet needs a quality cleaning utilizing the benefits of both systems.

The material of the carpet and the nature of the soiling are also factors in determining the proper cleaning method. In any case, our experienced technicians will perform a thorough inspection, including determining the fiber type to determine the proper course of action and help make the best decision for your home.

Our Process Nearly Eliminates Returning Spots

Carpet spots return because of capillary action. The carpet gets wet down to the backing with traditional cleaning. Then, the top of the fibers dries first due to airflow and evaporation. As the tops dry and the bottoms stay damp, soil and moisture are carried up to the driest point, the top. Our low moisture fast drying system nearly eliminates this issue. In fact, this is the process used to fix that very issue with traditional steam cleaning.

Our Process Stays Cleaner Longer

Using the most up-to-date cleaning technology, we can remove more dry soil before getting the carpet wet and remove the soil using less water, reducing the amount of cleaning agents needed. Traditional cleaning solutions can leave a residue that might be sticky, causing rapid re-soiling.

Our cleaning solution emulsifies and removes this residue, and if left behind, will continue to clean over the next couple of vacuum cycles. Our low moisture cleaning agents are enhanced with AFT, a new textile protector that leaves the material with a positive charge that resists oily binders attaching to the fiber.

How Often Should I Clean My Carpet?

The age old question, how often does your carpet need to be cleaned? The honest answer is; it depends on the following:

  • What type of carpet is it?
  • How is it used?
  • Is it well cared for?

We recommend every 3-6 months cleaning on very high traffic or homes with pets. If your carpet has regular traffic, we suggest every 6 months to 1 year. Carpet manufacturers generally require every 12 to 18 months to maintain most manufacturers' warranties. We encourage you to refer to your purchase agreement.

Carpet Cleaning for Your Health

Your home's carpet acts as a filter to capture dust, dander, plant matter, debris, allergens, mold, and other airborne contaminants. Just like the filter in your air conditioner, it needs to be emptied occasionally. Cleaning your carpet will remove most indoor allergens and soils that are trapped in it.

The longer it is between cleanings, the more cleaning effort it takes to empty the filter. Not only is this expensive, but it brings additional cleaning agents into the home and environment and creates more wear and tear on the carpet.

Carpet Cleaning to Protect Your Investment

Your carpet will degrade over time with wear. Soil embedded in the carpet will speed this process. Each tiny particle of loose soil in your carpet will act as a piece of glass or sandpaper, scratching and abrading the fiber and causing visible damage. Sticky soils, oils, colored spots, and soaps continue to work their way into the carpet over time, attracting more soil while embedding themselves deeper in the fiber.

Frequent vacuuming and regular professional carpet cleaning will slow the signs of aging for your carpet and prolong its life. Carpets can last 2 to 3 times longer or more, even if you have a good carpet maintenance program.

What's in Carpet Dirt?

Believe it or not, the soil in carpet has been analyzed by carpet mills to understand better how to construct better carpet in the future. We benefit from studies in the carpet cleaning industry by knowing what types of contamination we are removing. Here are the top 10 soils in carpets:

  1. Sand, clay and other "gritty" materials
  2. Natural fibers such as lint from clothing
  3. Oil, Grease, Petroleum
  4. Human hair and skin particles
  5. Dust mites, fleas, other insects
  6. Organic soils and materials (minerals and soils from landscaping areas, for example)
  7. Gum, ink, paint
  8. Airborne carbon and automotive exhaust
  9. Spilled food or beverages

Apart from these soils, your carpets may also have bacteria, odor, pet waste, smoke from fireplaces, candles, cigarettes, or other things.
